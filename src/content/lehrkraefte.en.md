# For educators

The Storyboard-Creator helps students plan media projects in a structured way —
short film, explainer video, photo story, audio play, podcast, stop motion,
comic, social media clip, roleplay or their own formats. This page is for you as
an educator. A student-facing guide is available under **Help**.

## Classroom use

The app suits project and group work from about grade 5 upwards and works across
subjects (language, art, civics, science, computing, foreign languages). The
level of detail is selectable:

- **Simple** — only the essential fields. For younger groups or a quick start.
- **Standard** — a balanced set of fields. Suitable for most projects.
- **Pro** — all fields including shot size, camera movement and materials. For
  in-depth media work and older grades.

This lets you match the cognitive load to the group without switching tools.

## Suggested workflow

1. **Choose a format** and set up the project (name, topic, subject, group).
2. Fill in the **planning**: logline, objective, roles, resources — this sharpens
   the idea before the first scene.
3. Create **scenes** and describe image, voiceover and notes.
4. **Review**: the app flags empty scenes.
5. **Export** as a `.storyboard` file (editable later), PDF or printout;
   **present** for a beamer or screen.

## Feedback feature

The **Feedback** toggle in the top bar reveals a comment field per scene. You or
peers can give targeted feedback right on the storyboard. Comments are stored in
the `.storyboard` file and can be marked as done.

## Privacy

The app runs **entirely in the browser**. There are no accounts, no sign-in and
no server transfer — text and images stay on the device. This keeps data use
minimal. Still note:

- The automatic save applies to **one device and one browser** only. On shared or
  changing school devices, data is otherwise lost.
- Have students save a `.storyboard` file at the end of each session (e.g. to the
  school cloud). That file is the backup.
- Remind students not to use images of identifiable people without consent.

See **Privacy** in the footer for details.

## Accessibility

The footer offers three **appearances** (light, dark, high contrast) and three
**font sizes**. Drag-and-drop is keyboard operable and the interface is labelled
for screen readers. The presentation is controllable with arrow keys, space and
escape.

## Printing on iPads

Depending on the school device or MDM policy, the direct PDF download may be
restricted. In that case use **Print** and save as PDF from the print dialog.
Test the path on your devices beforehand.
